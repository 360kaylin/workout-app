Diet focus

Lean, not crash diet

  • Protein at every meal: chicken, fish, eggs, Greek yoghurt, tofu, beans, lentils, lean meat, or protein milk.
  • Carbs around training: oats, rice, potatoes, pasta, wraps, fruit, or wholegrain bread.
  • Vegetables or fruit daily, aiming for at least five portions across the day.
  • Keep the deficit small: fewer sugary drinks, alcohol, big snack portions, and takeaway extras.
  • Abs come from core training plus lower overall body fat. You cannot choose fat loss from only the stomach.

Safety

Train hard, recover hard

Warm up before every session. Stop for sharp pain, dizziness, chest pain, or anything that feels wrong. If you are under 18, pregnant, injured, or have a medical condition, avoid aggressive dieting and get personal guidance from a qualified professional.

Form rules

Make 9 lb feel heavy

  • Use slow lowering, pauses, and single-leg work before chasing more reps.
  • Finish sets with one or two reps still clean. Do not let form collapse for extra numbers.
  • For arms, squeeze the top of curls and triceps extensions for one full second.
  • For legs, keep the working foot rooted and control the bottom position.
  • For abs, exhale hard and keep the lower back controlled instead of rushing.
